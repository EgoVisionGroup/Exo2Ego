Abstract

AI personal assistants, deployed through robots or wearables, require embodied understanding to collaborate effectively with humans. Current Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) primarily focus on third-person (exocentric) vision, overlooking the unique aspects of first-person (egocentric) videos. Additionally, high acquisition costs limit data size, impairing MLLM performance. To address these challenges, we propose learning the mapping between exocentric and egocentric domains, leveraging the extensive exocentric knowledge within existing MLLMs to enhance egocentric video understanding. To this end, we introduce Ego-ExoClip, a synchronized dataset comprising 1.1M ego-exo clip-text pairs sourced from Ego-Exo4D. Our approach features a progressive training pipeline with three stages: Teacher Self-Preparation, Teacher-Student Guidance, and Student Self-Practice. Additionally, we propose a instruction-tuning data EgoIT to strengthen the model's instruction-following capabilities, along with the EgoBench benchmark for thorough evaluation. Extensive experiments across multiple egocentric tasks reveal that existing MLLMs perform inadequately in egocentric video understanding, while our model significantly outperforms these leading models.

Conclusion

In this paper, we aim to advance egocentric video understanding by leveraging extensive exocentric knowledge integrated with MLLMs. To achieve this, we first construct a synchronized first- and third-person video-text dataset, Ego-ExoClip, based on Ego-Exo4D. Next, we design a progressive three-stage training pipeline to learn the mappings between exocentric and egocentric domains. Additionally, to enhance instruction-following abilities for downstream tasks, we introduce the egocentric instruction data EgoIT. To comprehensively evaluate egocentric video understanding performance, we propose a new benchmark, EgoBench, derived from multiple sources. Experimental results validate that our Exo2Ego framework outperforms existing MLLMs.
